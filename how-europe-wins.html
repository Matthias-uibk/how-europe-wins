Quantum Optical Ground Station · Innsbruck, Austria

A telescope in the Alps, pointed at the stars — not to observe, but to communicate. This station sends and receives single photons to satellites in low Earth orbit, forging quantum-secure links through 600 km of atmosphere.

One photon pair
at a time.

In an optics lab in Innsbruck, a Sagnac interferometer pumps ultraviolet light through a nonlinear crystal — creating pairs of photons that are entangled: quantum-mechanically linked regardless of distance.

One photon goes to the ground station's detector. The other is sent up — through 600 km of turbulent atmosphere — to a satellite overhead. Together, they generate a cryptographic key that is physically impossible to intercept without detection.

This is not theory. The optical ground station is built. The tracking system locks onto satellites with microrad precision. The entangled photon source is running. Right now, from the Alps, photon by photon, part of Europe's quantum infrastructure is coming to life.
